# **Peruvian Psychedelic Cumbia & Rock**

Embark on a hypnotic voyage through Peru’s golden era of cumbia and psychedelic-infused tropic rhythms. Spun 100% on vinyl, this set weaves heartfelt ballads from Trujillo with Amazonian instrumental odysseys.

## **Tracklist Deep Dive**

### **Peruvian Cumbia & Psychedelic Explorations**

- **Manzanita y su Conjunto – No Me Marcharé (2021)**  
  A soulful opener with warm shaker patterns and plaintive vocals, evoking late-night dance halls in Trujillo.

- **Los Wembler’s De Iquitos – Mi Caprichito (2019)**  
  Jungle-inflected organ swirls and wah-wah guitar conjure visions of Amazonian rituals at dawn.

- **Manzanita y su Conjunto – Primavera 71 (with Los Cañeros) (2021)**  
  Bright horn stabs and interlocking guitar riffs paint a picture of springtime renewal on coastal dancefloors.

- **Martín López y sus Estrellas – Por Qué No Te Quedas (1971)**  
  A heartfelt plea over lush strings and muted trumpet, showcasing the balled roots of Peruvian dance music.

- **Grupo Siglo XX de Rioja – Moyobambina (2022)**  
  An instrumental excursion with layered percussion and echo-drenched guitar, transporting you into a sonic rainforest.

- **Ranil – Baila Bonito (2022)**  
  Light, airy flute melodies dance atop syncopated rhythms, inviting you to surrender to the groove.

- **Los Beta 5 – Beteando (1969)**  
  Tight horn lines and punchy bass grooves exemplify funk-tinged cumbia from Peru’s late-’60s heyday.

- **Los Átomos de Paramonga – El Trencito (1969)**  
  Propulsive conga rolls and sharp guitar licks simulate the momentum of a speeding train.

- **Los Hijos del Sol – Cariñito (2017)**  
  Ángel Aníbal Rosado’s classic heartbreak anthem, centered on catchy chorus hooks and celebratory brass flourishes.

- **Los Sander’s de Ñaña – El Tramboyito (1969)**  
  Infectious call-and-response vocals and driving percussion keep the energy sky-high.

- **Grupo Celeste – Canción del maestro (1975)**  
  Reflective melodies and smooth production highlight cumbia’s soulful, introspective side.

- **Los Mirlos – Cabalgando Con Ella (1969)**  
  Galloping guitar solos and swirling percussion showcase the pioneers of Peruvian cumbia psych.

- **Los Ilusionistas – Colegiala (2017)**  
  The original iteration of the global hit, complete with warm organ riffs and unforgettable hooks.

- **Los Orientales de Paramonga – Lobos al Escape (1969)**  
  “Wolves on the Run” features playful guitar motifs and crisp percussion for a dancefloor romp.

- **Tono y sus Sicodelicos – El Guayacol (1968)**  
  Raw, garage-psychedelic textures and echo-laden vocals hint at nocturnal jam sessions in Lima.

- **Los Destellos – Cumbia del Desierto (2016)**  
  Sparse arrangements and haunting melodies evoke windswept dunes under a desert sun.

- **Los Destellos – Onsta la Yerbita (2016)**  
  Hypnotic, trance-like layering of guitars and bass invites a meditative, dance-inducing state.

- **Traffic Sound – Meshkalina (1970)**  
  Fuzz-drenched guitar and vibrant organ interplay deliver a full psych-rock detour.

- **Traffic Sound – La Camita (1971)**  
  A tender psych-soul ballad with piano-led motifs and lush vocal harmonies that linger like sunset light.

- **We All Together – It’s A Sin To Go Away (1973)**  
  Soft-rock sunshine pop meets introspective lyrics, closing the set on a wistful, reflective note.
