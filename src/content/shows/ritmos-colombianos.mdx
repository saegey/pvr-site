# Ritmos Colombianos

This vinyl-only set explores the rich musical terrain of Colombia — a country where cumbia, Afro-Caribbean rhythms, tropical psych, and modern Latin fusion collide. Spanning nearly seven decades, these tracks showcase Colombia’s enduring sonic pulse, from traditional dancefloor roots to kaleidoscopic experimentations.

Each record was handpicked for its rhythmic strength, historical weight, or wild creativity — and all spun direct from wax.

## The Journey

We begin in the golden era of Colombian cumbia and gaita, ease into salsa-heavy grooves, and transition through 21st-century psych and Afro-Latin experiments. The set finishes with bass-heavy, future-minded rhythms from the new Latin underground.

## The Track Rundown

### Antolín & Combo Orense – El Pájaro Milano (1965)

A bright, flute-heavy opener — celebratory and rooted in traditional Colombian cumbia.

### Peregoyo y Su Combo Vacana – Así Es Mi Tierra (1989)

This track brings Afro-Pacific vibrancy with bold vocals and unmistakable marimba swing.

### La Sonora del Caribe – Gaita Colombiana (1962)

Traditional gaita instrumentation layered over booming brass — deep, earthy roots music.

### Los Golden Boys – Las Colombianas (1970)

Joyful, guitar-led cumbia that evokes dances at coastal parties. Pure Colombian sunshine.

### Orquesta Nunez – La Samaria (1968)

A reissue from Colombia’s golden era — full of horn punches and tropical swagger.

### Afrosound – La Danza de los Mirlos (1973)

Jungle drums, guitar twang, and hypnotic repetition mark this instrumental classic.

### Wganda Kenya – Tifit Hayed (1975)

Funk meets Afro-Caribbean chant in this Colombian take on Afrobeat before it was cool.

### Los Corraleros de Majagual – El Mondongo (1965)

A gritty, fast-paced salsa jam loaded with percussion and old-school party vibes.

### Ondatrópica – El Caimán Y El Gallinazo (2012)

Psychedelia, cumbia, and analog funk meet in this genre-warping project by Mario Galeano.

### Cumbia en Moog – Cumbia Sobre el Mar (2019)

A haunting instrumental remake of the cumbia classic — analog synths float over deep groove.

### La Sonora Mazurén – Conciencia al Escape (2024)

Dark cumbia noir from Bogotá’s psychedelic underground — cinematic and tense.

### Meridian Brothers – Entra El Ritmo Antillano (2017)

Satirical and surreal, this is the Caribbean as seen through a hall of funhouse mirrors.

### La Sonora Mazurén – Alucinación (2024)

A woozy, warped, synth-heavy slow-burner with haunting melodic drift.

### Meridian Brothers & Conjunto Media Luna – La Pesadilla (2021)

A nightmare you’ll want to dance to — horns clash with rhythm in whimsical disarray.

### Romperayo & DJ Tudo – Guaracha Campesina Proletaria (2021)

Political, percussive, and gloriously messy — an Afro-Colombian samba stew.

### Tony Allen & La BOA – CUENTO (feat. Bejuco) (2025)

Afrobeat legend Tony Allen meets Bogotá funk and spoken word in this cross-Atlantic story.

### M.A.K.U SoundSystem – Contra Tambor (2019)

Blistering drums and bass from the Colombian diaspora in NYC — fiery and infectious.

### Frente Cumbiero – Cumbia Del Asilo (2020)

An epic closer — hypnotic, echo-drenched cumbia for forward-thinking dancers.

## Final Thoughts

Ritmos Colombianos is a vinyl time capsule and future shock all in one. These grooves reflect Colombia's depth, resilience, and endless creativity — from rural cumbia to urban experimentation.
