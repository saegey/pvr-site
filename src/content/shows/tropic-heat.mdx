# Analog Rhythms Vol. 2 – Tropic Heat

This vinyl-only set brings together high-energy sounds from Latin America, Africa, and the diaspora. It’s a sweaty, sun-baked journey through cumbia, psychedelic guitar grooves, Afrobeat, highlife, and rhythm-forward electronics—designed for dance floors and deep listeners alike.

Every track was carefully selected for its drive, warmth, and movement. Several were acquired in the past few months, reflecting the evolving shape of my crate and a deepening curiosity for global rhythm traditions.

<ResponsiveYouTube videoId={'9v_2Z-ns3RI'} />

## The Journey

We kick things off in the Amazonian heat with Sonido Verde de Moyobamba’s _La Cervecita_, before moving through Colombian psychedelia, futuristic takes on cumbia, and highlife grooves from Ghana. The set ebbs and flows with subtle shifts: vintage blends into contemporary, percussion gives way to melody, and back again.

## The Track Rundown

### Sonido Verde de Moyobamba – La Cervecita (1972)

Taken from the Analog Africa compilation _Perú Selvático_, this chicha instrumental is pure jungle groove. With surfy guitars and hypnotic rhythm, it’s a classic Peruvian Amazonian sound.

### Ondatrópica – Punkero Sonidero (2012)

From their self-titled LP, this track blends cumbia, punk attitude, and analog textures into one infectious jam. Ondatrópica bridges Colombia’s musical heritage with future-facing experimentation.

### Meridian Brothers – Cumbia de la Amistad (2020)

Part of their _Cumbia Siglo XXI_ album, this one’s a playful, off-kilter take on cumbia, filled with wobbly synths and tongue-in-cheek nostalgia.

### Chancha Vía Circuito – Barú (2018)

This deep, slow-burning digital cumbia builds tension through minimal percussion and mystical Andean textures. It’s rooted yet futuristic.

### Bomba Estéreo – Amar Así (2017)

Known for their genre-blending sound, Bomba Estéreo brings dreamlike vocals and electronic production on this love-drenched dance track from _Ayo_.

### Prince of Queens – Mérida (2024)

A fresh addition to the set, this track pairs dembow and house with deep tropical bass, offering a modern twist on global dance.

### Chicha Libre – Camintos de mi pueblo (Remix) (2020)

A playful remix with La Sonora Mazurén, blending Ecuadorian folk with psychedelic vibes and gritty textures. Released in 2024 but channeling a vintage spirit.

### Los Mirlos – Sonido Amazonico (1978)

Classic Amazonian cumbia from the legendary Los Mirlos, this track evokes jungle nights and romantic nostalgia.

### Los Orientales de Paramonga – Lobos al Escape (1969)

A raw and fuzzy Peruvian cumbia jam with garage-rock influence—short, punchy, and totally danceable.

### Los Destellos – Guajira Sicodélica (1968)

One of the earliest examples of psychedelic cumbia. Fuzz guitars, Latin percussion, and wah-wah effects define this iconic cut.

### Frente Cumbiero & Mad Professor – Bestiales 77 Dub (2010)

A dub-infused version that blends Colombian rhythms with deep studio echo and reverb from dub master Mad Professor.

### Meridian Brothers & Conjunto Media Luna – Mi primo el Boxeador (2021)

Quirky and narrative-driven, this track tells a whimsical story with off-kilter horns and rhythm. Part of the _Paz en la Tierra_ LP.

### Karen y Los Remedios – Flama Eterna (2023)

Psychedelic bolero meets synth-laden pop in this experimental piece from Mexico’s underground tropical scene.

### Romperayo & DJ Tudo – La Macumba (2021)

A Brazilian-Colombian collaboration that mixes samba-reggae with spaced-out effects and folkloric percussion.

### Punta Diamante – Afrodita vs. Tongolele (2020)

Sprawling and cinematic, this track merges Afro-Latin jazz with avant-garde textures and polyrhythmic percussion.

### The Apagya Showband – Kwaku Ananse (1974)

Highlife meets Afro-funk with deep grooves and infectious horn arrangements. A reissue of a Ghanaian gem.

### Greenwood Rhythm Coalition – Jewels (2019)

Clocking in at over 8 minutes, this extended Afrobeat groove builds patiently, layering hand percussion and swirling synths.

### Pedrinho – Odio Sem Valor (1983)

From _Space Echo_, this Cape Verdean synth-funk tune oozes soul and analog warmth. It’s a rare groove dance floor dream.

## Final Thoughts

This set is built for late-night dancing, tropical climates, and crate diggers who love the intersection of past and present. Whether it's rare reissues or new releases with vintage soul, Tropic Heat lives up to its name.
