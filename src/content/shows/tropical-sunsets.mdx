## **Tracklist Deep Dive**

### **Tropical & Global Grooves**

- **Ed Longo & The Applied Arts Ensemble – Love On the Line (2019)**  
  A buoyant blend of Afrobeat-infused horns and tight percussion, anchored by soulful vocals that float over a sun-kissed groove.

- **Parbleu – Venùs S’Endort Et Moi Avec Elle (2020)**  
  Dreamy electronic textures entwine with nostalgic disco rhythms, conjuring a hazy, late-night tropical reverie.

- **Nu Genea – 'A Voce 'E Napule (2018)**  
  A modern reinterpretation of classic Neapolitan songcraft, layered with subtle synths and breezy percussion that evoke coastal breezes.

- **Voilaaa – Jungle Fire (2015)**  
  Raw percussion and vibrant brass accents fuel a driving groove that sparks a wild, rainforest-inspired dancefloor energy.

- **El Combo Chimbita – Puro Show (2014)**  
  Electric cumbia meets psychedelic flourishes, blending raw percussion and neon synths in a high-energy tropical celebration.

- **Paolo Petrella (feat. Paolo Batà Bianconcini) – Maruzzella (feat. Paolo Batà Bianconcini) (2023)**  
  A modern take on classic Italian melody, with laid-back guitar lines and gentle rhythms that conjure seaside sunsets.

- **Vieux Farka Touré & Khruangbin – Diarabi (2022)**  
  Hypnotic desert blues rhythms meld seamlessly with Khruangbin’s languid, reverb-soaked guitar, creating a transcendent, sun-dappled mood.

- **Ebo Taylor – Peace on Earth (1977)**  
  A timeless Afrobeat gem featuring driving basslines, punchy horns, and Taylor’s warm vocals—an ode to unity under tropical skies.

- **Nu Genea (feat. Tony Allen) – NG Theme (feat. Tony Allen) (2016)**  
  Classic Afrobeat meets modern production as Tony Allen’s legendary drumming anchors an infectious, sunlit groove.

- **Pellegrino (feat. Zodyaco) [Migliera version] – Caucciù (feat. Zodyaco) [Migliera version] (2020)**  
  Deep bass grooves and off-kilter percussion combine with ethereal vocals, forging a mysterious, late-evening tropical soundscape.

- **Azymuth – Morning (1975)**  
  Smooth Brazilian jazz-funk licks drift over shimmering keys and crisp drum patterns, like dawn breaking over a tropical lagoon.

- **Claudia – Mas Que Nada (1971)**  
  A rare funk-infused cover of the Brazilian classic, complete with lush arrangements and steamy, danceable rhythms.

- **Zé Roberto – Lotus 72 D (1973)**  
  Psychedelic fusion meets Brazilian grooves—whirling guitars, laid-back drums, and cosmic keys propel this jungle-tinged trip.

- **Di Melo – Pernalonga (Mixed) (2021)**  
  A reimagined classic of Brazilian soul, merging vintage vocals and modern basslines to evoke hazy, tropical afternoons.

- **Romperayo – Que Viva la Vida y Que Muera la Muerte (2015)**  
  Raw cumbia energy with haunting vocal chants and percussive cascades, capturing both celebration and reverence under the sunset.

- **Los Locos del Ritmo – Llorona (1969)**  
  A gritty, vintage rock-meets-cumbia rendition of the folk classic, driven by driving percussion and reverb-drenched guitars.

- **Juaneco Y Su Combo – Ya Se Ha Muerto Mi Abuelo (2016)**  
  Psychedelic Amazonian cumbia with euphoric organ lines and rubbery bass, an intoxicating ride through jungle folklore.

- **Tony Allen & La BOA – LA MAQUINA DE TONY (2025)**  
  A futuristic afrobeat odyssey where Allen’s masterful drumming locks into hypnotic synth sequences and resonant grooves.

- **Adrian Quesada & Gabriel Garzón-Montano – El Paraguas (2022)**  
  Sultry, late-night tropical house vibes anchored by smooth bass, subtle keys, and Garzón-Montano’s silky vocals.

- **Hermanos Gutiérrez – Barrio Hustle (2024)**  
  High-octane guitar riffs weave through Latin rock rhythms, evoking sunlit city streets and dusty coastal highways.

- **Nicola Cruz – Cumbia del Olvido (2015)**  
  Deep, nocturnal cumbia pulses with electronic flourishes and earthy percussion, like moonlight filtering through palm fronds.

- **Øro – Sasà (2018)**  
  A laid-back tropical fusion where gentle guitar melodies float over hypnotic guembri bass and shuffling percussion.

- **Ed Longo & The Applied Arts Ensemble – Arcadian Dream (2019)**  
  Ethereal horns and cascading strings ride atop a mellow Afro-jazz foundation, painting a dreamlike tropical panorama.
