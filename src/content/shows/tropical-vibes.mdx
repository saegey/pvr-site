# Tropical Vibes Live @ Rapha Seattle

Join selectors Topyen and Saegey for an al fresco odyssey through cumbia, salsa, Afrobeat, Brazilian jazz, and more—brought to you live from the Rapha Seattle clubhouse on the longest day of the year, June 20th, 6–9 PM.

<ResponsiveYouTube videoId={"6F31lecKpfQ"} />

## Tracklist Deep Dive

### Global Tropical Grooves

- **Compay Segundo** – Me Dirás Qué Sabroso (2000)  
  A breezy blend of son and tropical guitar, this opener’s warm tres licks and vibrant horns instantly welcome you to the dancefloor.

- **Willie Colón & Héctor Lavoe** – Ché Ché Colé (1972)  
  Propulsive trombone riffs and Lavoe’s charismatic vocals drive this salsa classic like a late-night Latin dancehall anthem.

- **El Gran Combo de Puerto Rico** – Coro Coro Coro (2021)  
  Contemporary salsa powerhouse: tight brass arrangements and vibrant percussion reverberate across every rooftop.

- **Tito Puente** – Salsa Y Sabor (1972)  
  The king of timbales delivers crisp drum patterns and shimmering flute motifs, spicing up our terrace set with irresistible Latin jazz energy.

- **Meridian Brothers & El Grupo Renacimiento** – Poema del salsero resentido (2022)  
  A modern twist on old-school salsa, complete with playful horn stabs and unexpected rhythmic turns.

- **Ray Barretto** – Power (1970)  
  Soulful Latin jazz at its peak, Barretto’s congas lay down a deeply funky groove under muted trumpet lines.

- **Bad Bunny** – BAILE INoLVIDABLE (2025)  
  Fresh reggaeton-infused tropical pop, its catchy vocal hooks and pulsing electronic beats bring a contemporary club vibe.

- **Los Golden Boys** – Las Colombianas (1968)  
  Nostalgic cumbia with warm accordion melodies and driving bass, evoking coastal dancehalls in Colombia.

- **Elia y Elizabeth** – Alegría (1973)  
  Uplifting duo vocals and crisp percussion radiate pure celebration—sunshine in musical form.

- **Los Mirlos** – Lamento en la Selva (1969)  
  Psychedelic guitar echoes weave through classic cumbia rhythms, painting a sonic jungle tapestry.

- **Los Destellos** – Volando Con los Destellos (2016)  
  Shimmering psych-cumbia riffs and echo-drenched production propel you sky-high.

- **Grupo Celeste** – Perdí una flor (Original Master Tapes) (1975)  
  A bittersweet cumbia ballad with lush strings and a gentle sway that tugs at the heart.

- **Paolo Petrella** – Sarracino (feat. Paolo Batà Bianconcini) (2023)  
  Vintage swing meets modern cumbia, the playful vocals and swinging rhythm enchant every ear.

- **Chicha Libre** – Primavera en la Selva (2008)  
  Darkly melodic chicha grooves with buzzing guitar and a steady beat, conjuring dawn in the Amazon.

- **Quirino Do Canto** – Mino Di Mama (2016)  
  Hypnotic West African-infused melody driven by hand drums and call-and-response vocals.

- **La Banda Africana** – Te Clavo La…Mano (2020)  
  Afro-Colombian rhythms powered by bold percussion and rhythmic horns that energize the space.

- **Juaneco Y Su Combo** – Linda Nena (2016)  
  Amazonian cumbia at its most enchanting, with entrancing flute motifs and steady conga pulses.

- **Gal Costa** – Relance (1973)  
  Sultry MPB grooves, Costa’s velvet vocals gliding over a laid-back samba rhythm.

- **Rosa Maria** – Samba Maneiro (2017)  
  Upbeat Brazilian samba with crisp guitar patterns and an infectious chorus that lights up any corner.

- **Polibio Mayorga** – El Ferrocarril (1976)  
  Traditional Peruvian cumbia mimics a train’s motion with rolling percussion and rhythmic guitar licks.

- **Samba Negra** – Eberebijara (2020)  
  Deep Afro-Latin fusion with haunting chants and layered percussion for a trance-like journey.

- **Orti e Mayorga y Chiriboga** – Di Que Me Amas (2023)  
  Contemporary South American cumbia with bright synth accents and heartfelt vocals.

- **King Somalia** – Monkey’s Dance (2020)  
  Afrobeat-inflected rhythms and funky guitar curls that call everyone to the floor.

- **Los bere bere del Peru** – Dile a Mi Amada (1992)  
  Classic Peruvian tropical pop, nostalgic brass swells riding atop warm organ undertones.

- **Wganda Kenya** – Fiebre de Lepra (1965)  
  Raw garage-psych with swirling guitars and pounding drums, echoing mid-century tropical dissent.

- **Gilberto Gil** – Quilombo, O El Dorado Negro (1988)  
  MPB legend Gil fuses reggae, samba, and Brazilian folk into a rich tapestry of global sounds.

- **Tame Impala** – Endors Toi (2012)  
  Psychedelic rock with shimmering synths and dream-like vocals, bridging continents with hallucinatory grooves.

- **Adrian Quesada & Tita** – El Muchacho De Los Ojos Tristes (2022)  
  Latin soul meets jazz-funk horns and sultry vocals, perfect for late-night reflection.

- **Santana** – Oye Como Va (1970)  
  Timeless rock-salsa fusion, electrifying guitar solos and organ lines that ignite any crowd.

- **Malphino** – Octopus (2023)  
  Modern Latin electronica—pulsing beats and sleek guitar licks for a moody, dancefloor-ready cut.

- **Sonia López** – Polvo (2020)  
  Hypnotic cumbia with repetitive vocal hooks and subtle percussion that lulls and grooves.

- **Conjunto Papa Upa** – Chicharrón Peluo (2019)  
  Playful tropical funk with crunchy guitar tones and stop-start rhythms that surprise.

- **Silvestre Montez y Sus Guantanameros** – El Avispón (1968)  
  A classic Peruvian tropical cut, with jangly guitar lines and driving percussion that buzzes like an evening in the jungle.

- **Jorge Ben Jor** – Ponta de Lanca Africano (1976)  
  Brazilian grooves fused with funk and Afrobeat, his playful guitar dancing atop syncopated percussion.

- **Paolo Petrella** – Maruzzella (feat. Paolo Batà Bianconcini) (2023)  
  A bittersweet Italian-cumbia cover melding Mediterranean melodies with tropical groove.

- **Chicha Libre** – Sonido Amazonico (2008)  
  Atmospheric chicha with lush organ and steady rhythm, evoking misty forest soundscapes.

- **Tony Palkovic** – Born With a Desire (2018)  
  Modern jazz-tropical fusion in softly plucked guitar and mellow horn samples for sunset moods.

- **Azymuth** – Young Embrace (1979)  
  Brazilian jazz-funk at its peak—electric keys and bass lines that lift and swing effortlessly.

- **Gal Costa** – Pontos de Luz (1973)  
  A second dose of Costa’s MPB magic, soulful vocals entwined with lilting samba arrangements.

- **Chakachas** – Jungle Fever (1971)  
  Funky Latin percussion and sultry brass blend into a spicy groove that oozes nocturnal heat.

- **Voilaaa** – Bark (2015)  
  French Afro-pop bringing playful rhythms and chant-like vocals—a global twist on tropical themes.

- **Dirty Channels & Eternal Entropy** – Slaves (Trus’me Slave to the Rhythm Mix) (2016)  
  Electronic dance with tribal beats and swirling samples that hypnotize the senses.

- **Diogo Strausz** – 50 Anos Em 5 (Extended) (2021)  
  An extended tropical-house jam with lush pads and rhythmic guitar, closing the set on a euphoric high.
